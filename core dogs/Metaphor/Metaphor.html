Metaphor

A metaphor is a figure of speech that is used to make a comparison between two things that aren't alike but do have something in common. Unlike a simile, where two things are compared directly using like or as, a metaphor's comparison is more indirect, usually made by stating something is something else.

For example, take the language used to describe a debate:

A tree is a reasonable metaphor for how neurons look.
Trees have branches and branching roots, and neurons have dendrites and branching axons.
But the most obvious similarities end at the structural level. The functional level is a different matter.

A metaphor is a word or phrase used to describe something as if it was something else. A metaphor isn't a comparison – that's a simile, where you say one thing is 'like' another (“Her eyes were like diamonds”). Instead, a metaphor is simply a statement where you are saying that one thing is another.
